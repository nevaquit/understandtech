What's the difference between a VPC and a subnet? I keep confusing them.
Great question — this trips up a lot of people. Think of it this way:

A VPC (Virtual Private Cloud) is your entire private network — like buying a plot of land. A subnet is how you divide that land into zones (front yard, back yard, etc.).

Your VPC defines the overall IP range (e.g., 10.0.0.0/16). Subnets carve out smaller ranges within it — a public subnet (10.0.1.0/24) for web servers, a private subnet (10.0.2.0/24) for databases.
