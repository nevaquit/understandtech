Exam objective 4.2

Explain the security implications of proper hardware, software, and data asset management

Effective asset management is crucial for security, as it ensures that all hardware, software, and data assets are accounted for, secured, and properly disposed of throughout their lifecycle.

Asset Management Processes

Visual Representation: Asset Lifecycle Security

The following diagram maps security touchpoints across the asset lifecycle.

📦 Hardware, Software, and Data Asset Lifecycle
Acquisition
Security requirements in procurement; approved vendors only.
Assignment
Ownership, classification, and accountability tracking.
Monitoring
Inventory, enumeration, and unauthorized device detection.
Disposal
Sanitization, destruction, and certificate of destruction.
  • Acquisition/Procurement Process: Integrating security requirements into the purchasing process for new hardware and software to ensure that only secure and approved assets are brought into the environment.
  • Assignment/Accounting:
    • Ownership: Clearly defining who is responsible for each asset, including its security.
    • Classification: Categorizing assets based on their sensitivity and criticality to determine appropriate security controls.
  • Monitoring/Asset Tracking:
    • Inventory: Maintaining an accurate and up-to-date inventory of all hardware, software, and data assets.
    • Enumeration: Discovering and identifying all assets on the network, including unauthorized devices.
  • Disposal/Decommissioning: Securely removing assets that are no longer needed to prevent data leakage or unauthorized access.
    • Sanitization: Overwriting or degaussing data on storage media to make it unrecoverable.
    • Destruction: Physically destroying storage media (e.g., shredding, pulverizing) to ensure data cannot be recovered.
    • Certification: Obtaining certification that data has been securely sanitized or destroyed.
    • Data Retention: Policies defining how long data must be kept for legal, regulatory, or business purposes, and when it can be securely disposed of.
