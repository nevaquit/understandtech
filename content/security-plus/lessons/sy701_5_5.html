Explain types and purposes of audits and assessments

Audits and assessments are systematic evaluations of an organization's security posture, controls, and compliance with established standards. They help identify weaknesses and ensure that security measures are effective.

Audit and Assessment Types

  • Attestation: A formal statement or report by an independent third party confirming that an organization's security controls meet specific criteria.
  • Internal: Audits conducted by an organization's own employees or internal audit department.

- Compliance: Assessing adherence to internal policies, industry standards, or regulatory requirements. - Audit committee: A committee responsible for overseeing the internal audit function. - Self-assessments: Internal evaluations performed by the department or team responsible for the system.

  • External: Audits conducted by independent third parties.

- Regulatory: Audits mandated by government regulations. - Examinations: Detailed reviews of specific areas or controls. - Assessment: A broader evaluation of security posture. - Independent third-party audit: An audit performed by an external, unbiased organization.

  • Penetration Testing: Simulating real-world attacks to identify vulnerabilities and assess the effectiveness of security controls.

- Physical: Attempting to gain unauthorized physical access to facilities or systems. - Offensive: Actively attempting to exploit vulnerabilities. - Defensive: Focusing on the organization's ability to detect and respond to attacks. - Integrated: Combining offensive and defensive techniques. - Known environment (White Box): The penetration tester has full knowledge of the target system. - Partially known environment (Gray Box): The penetration tester has some limited knowledge of the target system. - Unknown environment (Black Box): The penetration tester has no prior knowledge of the target system. - Reconnaissance: The initial phase of penetration testing, gathering information about the target. - Passive: Gathering information without directly interacting with the target (e.g., OSINT). - Active: Gathering information by directly interacting with the target (e.g., port scanning).
