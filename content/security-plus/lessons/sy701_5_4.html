Exam objective 5.4

Summarize elements of effective security compliance

Security compliance involves adhering to laws, regulations, standards, and policies related to information security. Effective compliance ensures that an organization meets its legal and ethical obligations and protects sensitive data.

Compliance Management

  • Compliance Reporting: Documenting and reporting on an organization's adherence to security requirements.
    • Internal: Reports generated for internal stakeholders to track compliance status.
    • External: Reports submitted to regulatory bodies, auditors, or clients to demonstrate compliance.
  • Consequences of Non-compliance: Understanding the potential penalties for failing to meet compliance requirements.
    • Fines: Monetary penalties imposed by regulatory bodies.
    • Sanctions: Official actions taken against an organization for non-compliance.
    • Reputational damage: Negative impact on an organization's public image and trustworthiness.
    • Loss of license: Revocation of operating licenses or certifications.
    • Contractual impacts: Breach of contract with clients or partners due to non-compliance.
  • Compliance Monitoring: Continuously overseeing an organization's activities to ensure ongoing adherence to compliance requirements.
    • Due diligence/care: Exercising reasonable care and effort to meet security obligations.
    • Attestation and acknowledgement: Formal statements or confirmations of compliance.
    • Internal and external: Monitoring performed by internal teams and external auditors.
    • Automation: Using tools and scripts to automate compliance checks and reporting.
  • Privacy: Protecting personal information and adhering to privacy regulations.
    • Legal implications: Understanding the legal requirements related to data privacy.
      • Local/regional: Privacy laws specific to a particular city or region.
      • National: Privacy laws applicable across a country (e.g., CCPA in the US).
      • Global: International privacy regulations (e.g., GDPR).
    • Data subject: The individual to whom personal data relates.
    • Controller vs. processor:
      • Controller: Determines the purposes and means of processing personal data.
      • Processor: Processes personal data on behalf of the controller.
    • Ownership: Clear definition of data ownership and responsibility.
    • Data inventory and retention: Maintaining a record of all data assets and policies for how long data is kept.
    • Right to be forgotten: The right of individuals to have their personal data erased under certain circumstances.
