Exam objective 5.2

Explain elements of the risk management process

Risk management is the process of identifying, assessing, and controlling threats to an organization's capital and earnings. It involves understanding potential risks and implementing strategies to mitigate them.

Risk Management Process

  • Risk Identification: Discovering, recognizing, and describing risks. This involves identifying assets, threats, and vulnerabilities.
  • Risk Assessment: Evaluating the likelihood and impact of identified risks.

- Ad hoc: Assessments performed as needed. - Recurring: Regular, scheduled assessments. - One-time: Assessments for specific projects or events. - Continuous: Ongoing monitoring and assessment of risks.

  • Risk Analysis: Detailed examination of identified risks.

- Qualitative: Subjective assessment of risk based on expert judgment (e.g., high, medium, low). - Quantitative: Objective assessment of risk using numerical values and calculations. - Single Loss Expectancy (SLE): The monetary loss expected each time a risk event occurs (Asset Value x Exposure Factor). - Annualized Loss Expectancy (ALE): The expected monetary loss for an asset over a year (SLE x Annualized Rate of Occurrence). - Annualized Rate of Occurrence (ARO): The estimated frequency with which a threat is expected to occur in a year. - Probability: The likelihood of a risk event occurring. - Likelihood: The chance of a threat exploiting a vulnerability. - Exposure Factor: The percentage of asset value lost due to an incident.

  • Risk Register: A document that records all identified risks, their assessment, and planned responses.

- Key risk indicators: Metrics used to monitor and track risks. - Risk owners: Individuals responsible for managing specific risks. - Risk threshold: The maximum level of risk an organization is willing to tolerate.

  • Risk Tolerance: The acceptable level of variation around risk objectives.
  • Risk Appetite: The amount of risk an organization is willing to take to achieve its objectives.

- Expansionary: High risk appetite, willing to take significant risks for potential high rewards. - Conservative: Low risk appetite, prefers to avoid risks. - Neutral: Moderate risk appetite, balances risk and reward.

  • Risk Management Strategies:

- Transfer: Shifting the risk to another party (e.g., cyber insurance). - Accept: Acknowledging the risk and deciding to take no action to mitigate it, often because the cost of mitigation outweighs the potential impact. - Exemption: Formal approval to deviate from a security policy for a specific reason. - Exception: A temporary deviation from a security policy. - Avoid: Eliminating the risk by not engaging in the activity that carries the risk. - Mitigate: Implementing controls to reduce the likelihood or impact of a risk.

  • Risk Reporting: Communicating risk information to stakeholders.
  • Business Impact Analysis (BIA): A process that identifies and evaluates the potential effects of a disruption to critical business operations.

- Recovery Time Objective (RTO): The maximum tolerable duration of time that a computer, system, network, or application can be down after a disaster or disruption during which the recovery process must be completed. - Recovery Point Objective (RPO): The maximum tolerable amount of data that can be lost from an IT service due to a major incident. - Mean Time to Repair (MTTR): The average time required to repair a failed component or system. - Mean Time Between Failures (MTBF): The predicted elapsed time between inherent failures of a system during operation.
