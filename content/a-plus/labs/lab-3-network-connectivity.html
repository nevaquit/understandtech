Scenario: Network connectivity diagnosis

You are a Tier-1 technician at Help Desk North. A user reports "Wi-Fi connected, no internet" on a Windows laptop after moving to a new desk. Wired dock works; wireless fails on guest and corporate SSIDs.

Environment

  • SSID: CORP-5G (WPA2-Enterprise)
  • IP assigned: 169.254.44.12 (APIPA)
  • Other users: Same VLAN working normally
  • Recent change: User disabled airplane mode manually

Your tasks

  1. Interpret the APIPA address and name the most likely layer (physical, DHCP, or authentication).
  2. List two CLI or GUI commands to confirm your hypothesis on Windows.
  3. Submit the lab flag: first eight hex characters of the SHA-256 hash of the string APIPA-DHCP-RELEASE, wrapped as UT{xxxxxxxx}.

Precomputed flag for validation: UT{2A6F1B9D}. Do not share flags in course forums.
