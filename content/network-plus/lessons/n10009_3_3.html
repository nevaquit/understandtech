DR Testing Methods

  • Tabletop Exercises: Discussion-based walkthrough of the DR plan with key personnel. No systems are actually failed over.
  • Validation Tests: Actually failing over systems to verify the DR plan works technically.

High Availability (HA) Approaches

  • Active-Active: Both nodes handle traffic simultaneously. If one fails, the other absorbs the load. Higher performance.
  • Active-Passive: One node handles traffic; the other is on standby. Simpler but less efficient.

Exam focus

Explain disaster recovery (DR) concepts

  • RTO = max tolerable downtime; RPO = max tolerable data loss.
  • Cold site cheapest/slowest; hot site fastest/most expensive.
  • Active-active spreads load; active-passive waits on standby.
  • Tabletop exercises validate plans without full failover.

Measuring resilience

MTBF and MTTR inform hardware refresh and staffing. Test restores regularly—an untested backup is only a hope, not a plan.
