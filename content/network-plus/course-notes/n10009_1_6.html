Enterprise Network Architectures

  • Three-Tier Hierarchical Model:
  • Core Layer: High-speed backbone; connects distribution layers. No policy enforcement here.
  • Distribution Layer: Aggregates access layer switches; applies routing, policies, and QoS.
  • Access Layer: Where end devices (PCs, printers, phones) connect to the network.
  • Spine-and-Leaf: Two-tier data center architecture. Every leaf connects to every spine. Provides predictable, low-latency east-west traffic flow.
  • Collapsed Core: Core and distribution layers combined. Used in smaller networks.
  • Traffic Flows:
  • North-South: Traffic between internal network and external internet (client to server).
  • East-West: Traffic between servers within the same data center (server to server). Spine-and-leaf is optimized for this.

Pro tip

The shift from three-tier to spine-and-leaf was driven by the explosion of east-west traffic in virtualized data centers. Knowing this context helps answer scenario questions about modern data center design.
